The Story

The Bucket NYC (as I refer to it) is a recording facility unlike any I or my clients have ever encountered. The sun pours in through the windows and into the control room. The high cielings, sprawling floor plan, exposed brick, the iconic backdrop of Coney Island with its rides, boardwalk and ocean... they all create a comfortable, casual and creative envrionment that no other studio in NYC can offer. And conveniently, it's directly across the street from the N Q F and D subway lines.

There is absolutely no place like this in NYC, and it's low fixed costs make it viable fo even the most modest of dreamers. This space comes with a rich history of artists and producers who have come through it and has housed a working studio for over 13 years. It was originally the vision and product of producer Don Fury.

RENT or BUY a fully equipped Pro Tools HD studio that can easily bill upwards of $50/hr. Do the math, and get back to worrying about the music more than the money.

Offer 1: Own It.

The tenant in possession of the lease agreement with the landlord will be responsible to pay the landlord rent ($2600/mo minus a $300/mo discount for allowing access through the studio to the roof because, at times, it is needed).

The tenant is the owner of all property INSIDE of the loft - the furniture. studio gear, studio build-out, walls, floated floors, fixtures, appliances, sink, toilet etc. This means that all of the capital improvements to the studio remain under ownership of the tenant. When the lease expires, the tenant will still own all of that property, but they no longer have an agreement with the landlord to rent the space.

For the price of the turnkey buyout, I am including the rights to the lease, which is 5 years with each year guaranteeing the tenant the option to renew at a rent held at $2600 for five years ($2300 with discount). I am also including all gear, furnishings, fixtures etc. These will all become the permanent property of the buyer. Should the tenant want to vacate before the lease expires, they may sell the rights to another tenant to whom they want to transfer the lease.

Gear, Furnishings & Buildout: $125,000 or best offer.

The Owner

My name is Adam Richman. After a number of years of recording myself and friends, as well as touring as a solo-artist, guitar player and musical director for other artists, I moved into this space and set forth on a path to producing and co-writing songs for major labels and indie artists alike. This studio has enabled a type of work environment I had never imagined I'd be able to make real. I always wanted a place where the clock wouldn't be ticking, where the clients would feel at home, and where creativity could reign over other external pressures. That is exactly what I was able to achieve here.

In the last year, the circumstances of my personal life shifted greatly, and my creativity was awoken to other mediums. I've recently been offered an opportunity that I can't refuse, but it requires that I leave NYC. This is the ONLY reason that I would step away from this incredible space, and as a result, my loss will be next owner's significant gain.

Please, let's talk if this sounds like an opportunity to you, because it is. Big steps are a leap of faith, but this place is an exception to many of the hardships involved with building a studio, and business, from the ground-up.

It could be yours. Start today.
